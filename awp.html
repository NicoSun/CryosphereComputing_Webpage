The maximum accumulated solar radiation varies greatly by latitude when taken over the whole astronomical summer. At the north pole the max is 2500MJ/m2, at 75N it is 3000MJ/m2, at 60N 4000MJ/m2 and at 44N 5000MJ/m2. That's why on the map with raw energy values the Arctic appears significantly bluer than the south, even when regions are mostly icefree. Instead of energy values the chart on the left shows the percentage of locally avaliable solar radiation.
