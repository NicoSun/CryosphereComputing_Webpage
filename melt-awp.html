Ice Melting Arctic Albedo-Warming Potential

Test phase

The long term downwards trend is caused by reduced sea ice cover and a general northward movement of the melting regions. The input solar energy never changes in the model and can be considered constant in the real world as well. Greenhouse gases are not an extra energy input into the system. They only insulate it (in terms of radiation) against the coldness of space.

Supposedly high melt areas on the coastline are just coastline measurement errors. Instead of the typical 15% SIC used to classify a gridcell as ice covered this model uses 25% to reduce these measurement errors. They dominate late season values in mostly icefree seas.

Areas with low melt regions right next to high melt regions are areas where the sea ice was blown away by winds and currents. Even though they are icefree and accoumaled huge AWP anomalies the never had actual ice melting in this place. Notable recurring areas are southern Beaufort Sea, Laptev Sea, Northern Baffin Bay and Hudson Bay.

Large Areas with very high melt AWP have experienced ice melt throuout the summer. Winds and currents have pushed ice into these areas and have been a melting spot all the time. Notable are Northern Greenland Sea, Eastern-Central Beaufort Sea and Northern Greenland Sea.
